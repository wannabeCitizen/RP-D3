Journals

The way homework is assigned in our instructional model involves doing a series of challenges that step students through different aspects of the tool they are learning. These challenges start simple - e.g., downloading and installing the packages - and end with a fully functional use of the tool. In this way students are actively engaging with their skills by doing activities that integrate this new tool into their repertoire of abilities. All work is submitted in the way of a learning journal, which is described further below.

Each assignment also has a reflective compontent that asks students to give feedback about the content and their experience with our module. We also ask questions about peer learning and problem solving to get a sense of how people are learning together and what extraneous resources they used to help them complete the assignment. This feedback is collected in the learning journal by posing a series of questions at the end of each module we ask them to respond to prior to submission.

Our current design has the assignment deployed on our class website which displays a document walking students through the challenges they are to complete. Specific places where a submission is required are highlighted, which normally involves taking a screenshot or sharing a code snippet to prove the student has progressed to a certain point. These submissions as well as responses to reflective questions are gathered through the learning journal

which is a Google Presentation Document. Each week we put up a template document that has slide for each submission as well as a slide for every journal question.
